The Barre

No matter the dancer or where they are in development, it all starts with ballet class which begins with exercises at the barre. It is a ritual that takes place everyday throughout the career of the dancer. Toddlers just starting out, young teens in pre-professional programs, recreational dancers in open classes and Prima Ballerinas in world renowned professional companies around the world, all work at the barre to learn and profect their craft.

Pointe Shoes

The pointe shoe is an exciting rite of passage for many young girls dreaming of becoming professional ballet dancers. Depending on their emotional, physical and technical development, many young girls will begin pointe work between the ages of 9 and 11. It's an exciting time! It takes many years to build the strengh, artistry and technique that makes pointe work look so ethereal and effortless.

Company Class

Even with a day filled with hours of rehearsals and performances, daily company class is a staple in the lives of professional ballet dancers. Daily class helps the dancer gauge where their bodies are on a given day and prepare them for the day ahead. In additon to company class, dancers use various forms of crosstraining to keep their bodies in peak condition and to prevent injury. No wonder dancers are considered some of the very best atheletes in the world!

Corps De Ballet

Almost all professional ballet dancers will start their careers in the corp de ballet. The corps provides the "frame" and completes the look and feel of the ballet being performed. Dancing in the corps is some of the most difficult and challenging work that dancers do. In addition to their own corps work, dancers often understudy the roles of other corps dancers, as well as soloist roles. A Dancer may spend several years dancing in the Corps de Ballet before being promoted to Soloist or Principal dancer. Some dancers have very fulfilling careers while dancing in the corps the entirety of their careers.

The Stars

The highest ranked dancers in a company are the Principal dancers. These are the stars of the ballet who perform the lead roles and may be the most recognizable "faces" of a professional ballet company. Ballet is an extremely competitive field, and for some dancers, it may take years to climb the ranks to Principal Dancer. Some Principals, like Natalia Osipova and David Hallberg pictured performing in American Ballet Theatre's production of 'Giselle', become international stars beyond their home companies and go on to guest as Principal artist with many of the biggest and most well known ballet compnies in the world.
